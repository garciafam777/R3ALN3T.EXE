To make an expanded world with a 2,000-meter vertical split functional, the global system design cannot rely on localized infrastructure. It requires a massive, integrated planetary network capable of managing two extreme realities: the thin-aired, dry High Plateaus (old continents) and the crushing, humid Abyssal Basins (old seafloors).Here is the architectural and systemic framework for this new world system.1. The Planetary Transit Grid: Bridging the Vertical SplitStandard logistics fail when a trade route requires climbing a sheer 2-kilometer cliff face where air pressure drops by nearly half. The global transportation system is split into three primary tiers:Mag-Lev Cliff-Runners (The Incline Grid)The System: Massive, hyper-reinforced magnetic levitation tracks that run vertically up the continental shelves at $45^\circ$ to $60^\circ$ angles.Engineering: These tracks handle the brutal transit between the Abyssal Basins and the High Plateaus. The train cars are pressurized, sealed capsules resembling spacecraft. As they ascend, internal life-support systems gradually depressurize the cabins to prevent the passengers' lungs and equipment from experiencing explosive decompression sickness (the "bends").Heavy-Drop Flight CorridorsThe System: Air travel is entirely dictated by atmospheric density. Conventional aircraft cannot fly efficiently in both zones.The Solution: The world system utilizes Dual-Profile Atmospheric Shuttles. In the dense Abyssal Basins, they use small, high-efficiency wings. When ascending to the High Plateaus, they deploy variable-geometry swept wings or engage rocket-assisted vertical thrusters to maintain lift in the thin, alpine air.2. The Siphon Spire NetworkCollection: Located at the absolute lowest points of the world (the trench floors), massive automated industrial complexes pump the remaining hypersaline water into geothermal distillation plants.Distribution: The purified fresh water is forced upward against gravity using high-pressure, multi-stage pumping stations lined along the continental shelves. These pipelines feed the agricultural biomes on the old continents.Orographic Cloud CatchersThe System: On the edges of the High Plateaus, engineers construct massive, kilometers-wide mesh nets supported by carbon-fiber pylons.Function: As humid air from the Abyssal Basins is naturally pushed up the cliff faces by wind currents, it hits these nets. The moisture instantly condenses, creating a massive, reliable stream of clean water that is channeled into artificial rivers flowing inward across the dry continental interiors.3. Global Power Architecture: The Planetary Energy GridA larger, expanded world requires an energy infrastructure that exploits the planet's new physical layout.Geothermal Core CapsThe System: With the ocean floor exposed, tectonic fault lines and ancient mid-ocean ridges are easily accessible.Function: Linear networks of geothermal power plants—spanning thousands of miles along the old Atlantic and Pacific ridges—capture the raw heat of the mantle. This forms the baseline power grid for the entire planet, transmitting energy via ultra-high-voltage superconducting cables buried deep beneath the silt flats.Kinetic Wind HarvestersThe System: The 2,000-meter continental walls create massive, permanent wind tunnels as air cools and warms between the high and low zones.Function: The shelf edges are lined with rows of vertical-axis kinetic wind turbines. These turbines run 24/7 due to the non-stop atmospheric churning, providing localized power to the Cliff-Runner transit systems and border outposts.4. Administrative & Data Logistics: The "NetNavi" InfrastructureManaging a world this vast requires decentralized but instantaneous data transmission.The Silt-Line Network: Instead of vulnerable communication satellites looking through heavy, permanent basin cloud covers, global data is carried by Sub-Silt Fiber Trunks. These are heavily armored, self-healing data lines laid directly into the soft abyssal clay of the seafloor, linking the deep trench cities directly to the plateau capitals.High-Altitude Relay Spires: For wireless communication, towers are placed exclusively on the highest peaks of the old world (like the new Sky-Spine mountains in Puerto Rico). Sitting above the dense clouds and atmospheric distortion of the lowlands, these spires use high-frequency lasers to beam data across the plateaus and down into specific basin sectors.This three-tiered approach—vertical mag-levs, an engineered hydrology loop, and tectonic power grids—allows civilization to scale across the massive, newly exposed territory of 3026 without being isolated by the brutal geography.
