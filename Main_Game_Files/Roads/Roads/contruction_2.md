To pull off massive engineering feats in a world split by a 2,000-meter drop, the A.R.C.H.I.V.I.S.T. AI cannot work in a vacuum. It relies on a sprawling, multi-tiered economic engine driven by specialized career paths, extraction corporations, and high-risk logistics.In-game, these roles create a rich gameplay loop where truckers, corporate fixers, and miners directly feed the 60-day construction cycle.1. Key Career Roles & In-Game ImpactAbyssal Truckers & Logistics Pilots (The Haulers)Standard cargo hauling is a death-defying career when moving goods between the high and low worlds.The Job: Driving massive, heavily tracked Silt-Crawlers across the treacherous, shifting mud of the exposed seafloors, or piloting pressurized freight rigs down the $50^\circ$ Mag-Lev Incline tracks.Game Mechanics: Truckers must manage mechanical heat, tire pressure (which swells dramatically as they descend into the heavy 1.5 atm basin air), and cargo stability.Construction Impact: If a player completes a high-priority "Heavy Freight Run" safely through a dust storm, it acts as a massive resource injection, shaving 3 to 5 days off an active project's timer.Silt-Dredgers & Core Miners (The Extractors)The foundation of 3026 architecture relies entirely on materials that didn't exist when the oceans were full.The Job: Operating gargantuan automated mining rigs that scoop up hyper-dense abyssal silt or cut through the razor-sharp, calcified fossilized coral reefs. On the High Plateaus, miners extract rare volcanic crystals exposed by ancient deep-sea faults.Game Mechanics: Defending mining operations from environmental hazards (like tectonic steam vents bursting through the dry seabed) or competing corporate raiders.Construction Impact: Upgrading extraction infrastructure increases the passive building speed of all regional projects under the A.R.C.H.I.V.I.S.T. umbrella.Corporate Material Brokers (The Sourcers)Mega-corporations control the processing patents for turning raw seafloor crust into structural components.The Job: Corporate executives and freelance fixers negotiate massive supply contracts between the extraction guilds and the construction sites. They deal in specialized components like basalt-tempered smart glass and anti-corrosive polymer seals.Game Mechanics: The player interacts with corporate factions to secure blueprints and bulk material discounts. Choosing to back one corporation over another can alter the visual aesthetic of the finished city (e.g., sleek corporate minimalism vs. gritty, functional industrialism).Construction Impact: Securing a corporate contract satisfies the "Rare Earth" or "Advanced Alloy" material requirements for Phase 3 (Environmental Integration) of Mega-Scale projects.3. The Interactive Supply & Demand EconomyTo tie this all together into the game's simulation, the A.R.C.H.I.V.I.S.T. AI hosts a dynamic Project Procurement Board. This system ensures that the world feels interconnected and populated by working professions.4. NPC Story Integration: The Supply Line Quests
By interacting with NPCs within these career fields, the player drives the narrative of the city's expansion.

For example, a grizzled long-haul trucker named Mac-04 (a specialized Hauler NetP) might give you a quest line because his convoy was intercepted by raiders near the Puerto Rico Trench. If you clear the road and escort his rig carrying the geothermal core caps to the site, the A.R.C.H.I.V.I.S.T. AI triggers an immediate phase shift in the local city design, moving the project from a hollow skeleton (Phase 2) to a fully sealed, glowing hub (Phase 4) right before your eyes.
