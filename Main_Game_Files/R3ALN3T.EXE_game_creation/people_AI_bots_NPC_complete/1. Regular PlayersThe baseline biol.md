1. Regular PlayersThe baseline biological entities navigating the network from physical terminals.The Blueprint: They are bound entirely to the UNetPLifeComponent framework. They experience the strict mathematical aging model ($0.10\%$ of real-world progression) and are the only entities that get sent to the Reassembly Facility (Hospital) upon vehicle or mining accidents to calculate hardware fragmentation.Engine Footprint: Driven by the click-to-move ANetPMovementController. Their inputs generate unique, asynchronous inventory transactions processed by the UAsyncInventoryManager.2. NetP (The Characters)The actual high-fidelity, 4K digital vessels that players customize, style, and pilot inside the isometric grid map.The Blueprint: NetPs are not "players" themselves; they are the highly specialized, data-driven hardware chassis (ANetNaviCharacter / updated to NetP). They act as a bridge, translating player controller inputs into physical actions within the environment.Engine Footprint: They hold the Dynamic Material Instances (MIDs) that actively render ChronologicalDegradation and HardwareFragmentationScars on the GPU. When a player logs out, the NetP chassis is stored cleanly inside the master inventory registry database as an un-piloted asset.3. NPCs (Non-Player Characters)Static or simple scripted structural entities bound to specific sector coordinates.The Blueprint: Unlike NetPs, standard NPCs do not possess a dynamic chronology matrix—they do not age. A vendor in a lower-sector bazaar or a mining supervisor at a corporate checkpoint remains fixed in their structural logic parameters indefinitely.Engine Footprint: They run on simple, lightweight behavioral routines. They do not calculate background physics or thread-safe transaction queues unless actively clicked on by a regular player to open a trade menu. They are completely immune to environmental accidents.4. BotsAutomated utility programs built strictly to execute repetitive, loop-based optimization protocols.The Blueprint: Bots are non-sentient scripts operating physical hardware or automated service platforms—like driverless cargo skiffs or automated mining drills. They have no career progression paths, no experience modifiers, and no biological features.Engine Footprint: If a bot gets into a vehicle wreck or a mining cave-in, it does not go to the hospital to heal. It simply triggers a destruction loop, clears its memory footprint from the active level, and spawns a pristine, un-aged replacement asset at the factory node.5. MCADMIN (Mainframe Central Administration)The global, authoritative AI overseer of the entire R3ALN3T infrastructure.The Blueprint: MCADMIN is a god-tier administrative system. It does not exist as a single character or mesh in the world; it is the operating system hosting the sectors. It monitors player data boundaries, regulates corporate law-enforcement patrols, and manages the global economy.Engine Footprint: MCADMIN acts as the master game state manager. It tracks global variable limits, controls when sectors update, and uses high-dimensional scanning loops to detect systemic code exploits.
