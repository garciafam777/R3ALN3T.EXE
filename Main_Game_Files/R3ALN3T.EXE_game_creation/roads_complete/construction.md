Real-Time Construction Staging (Visual System)When a city expansion project begins, the area shouldn't just "pop" into existence. It progresses through dynamic visual phases to make the world feel alive and reactive.Phase 1: Surveying & Silt Stabilization (Day 1 – 5): Holographic grid lines fence off the zone. Heavy "Silt-Compactor" drones press down the abyssal mud or blast away jagged shelf coral. Yellow digital hazard signs blink around the perimeter warning citizens of high-pressure atmospheric venting.Phase 2: Coral-Crete Scaffolding (Day 6 – 30): Structural skeletons emerge. Carbon-fiber cranes lift modular blocks into place. The area is filled with NetP workers welding with plasma torches, creating ambient sparks visible from a distance.Phase 3: Environmental Integration (Day 31 – 50): Atmospheric pressure locks, condensation catchers, and heavy-duty basalt glass paneling are installed. The building looks 90% complete but remains wrapped in synthetic construction netting.Phase 4: Calibration & Reveal (Day 51 – 60): The scaffolding descends. The AI runs a calibration sequence, lighting up the neon bioluminescent signs, cycling the air locks, and opening the zoning barrier for NPCs to flood in.2. 4. In-Game Story IntegrationThe construction system is tied directly to the narrative. For example, if a faction wants to establish a new city path on the sheer vertical walls of the Caribbean Sky-Spine Range, the player must first deploy a specialized Anchor NetP to weld the initial foundation pylons into the cliffside.As the 60-day timer ticks down in the background of your playthrough, the region physically changes. A bare cliff face evolves into a bustling, tiered vertical hanging town, complete with working atmospheric locks and docking bays for Dual-Profile Atmospheric Shuttles, opening completely new quest lines and merchant inventories.
