Fuel unit and properties

Fuel: Bugmatter (net-spill organic-plasma synthesized into usable energy).
Unit: Flux Unit (FU). 1 FU ≈ energy of 1 liter of diesel (game-balance baseline).
Forms: Raw Slurry (unstable, needs refining), Refined Gel (stable, high-density), Bio-Sap (living-biofeed for biohybrids), Quantum Dust (ultra-high-energy for chrono/quantum drives).
Hazards: Raw Bugmatter bleeds reality (attracts microportals/phantoms). Refining requires containment and Portal-Safe Catalysts.

Portal mechanics and gameplay rules (summary)

Discovery: When a Bugmatter deposit is found, a portal manifests at the site. Portals range micro → macro and correspond to deposit size.
Collection rule: Portals must be sealed (closed) before raw Bugmatter can be safely harvested/removed; otherwise extraction causes bleed events (environmental hazards, AI echoes, spawn phantoms).
Closure process: Each portal requires a tailored Seal Routine: deploy specific Stabilizers (Anchors/Nets/Nodes/Chimera/Biolock/Chrono-Stabilizers) and run a sequence (time, resources, min tech level). Larger portals require multi-step fleet or civic operations.
Costs/time: Small micro-portals: single team, 5–15 minutes in-game, cost 10–50 FU-equivalent in Seal Charges. Mid portals: squad operation, 1–6 hours, cost 500–5,000 FU. Macro/stellar portals: multi-faction raid, days/weeks, cost 50k–1M FU and political permits.
Risk/reward: Sealed portals yield stable Bugmatter stores (refinery yields vary: raw → Refined Gel 65–90% efficiency; conversion to Quantum Dust requires high-end labs and fractional yields). Failure to seal yields corruption, mutated fauna, or permanent map hazards.
Infrastructure: Fuel stations require Refinery Nodes (to convert Raw Slurry → Gel/Bio-Sap/Quantum Dust). Some vehicles (biohybrids) can convert raw onsite if portal properly sealed and bioreactors synchronized.
Game mechanics hooks (optional rules)

Seal Skill checks (Engineering/Arcane/Netcraft) determine success and efficiency.
Territory control: factions that control portals gain exclusive harvest rights; sealing a portal can create a permanent Fuel Node until depleted.
Environmental events: unsealed portals cycle hazards that alter local resource spawn rates; players may choose riskier extraction for rarer Quantum Dust.

MID-SIZED OCEANIC PORTAL — PLAYER MISSION FLOW Mission Title: "Seal the Blossom — Mid Oceanic Portal" Objective: Close a mid-sized oceanic portal (5k–75k FU raw) and secure initial Bugmatter yield.
Mission Parameters

Recommended Team: 6 players (roles: Lead Engineer, Biologist, Marine Pilot, Diver/Operator, Security, AI/Net Specialist)
Required Items: Mobile Refinery Module (MRM), 1 Seal Array Kit (mid), 6 Stabilizer Anchors, 3 Biolock Nodes, 2 Pressure Dampers, 1 Fleet Beacon
Time budget: 6–12 game hours (shorter with upgrades)
Phase 0 — Recon & Lockdown

Deploy marine scout drone to mark portal coordinates; scan for portal radius and bleed vectors.
Establish perimeter: drop two Fleet Beacons to mark safe corridor and call for temporary no-fly marine zone.
Initial hazard: phantoms spawn; Security deploys suppressors.
Phase 1 — Anchor Placement & Stabilization

Marine Pilot anchors MRM at safe distance (200–500 m depending on portal intensity).
Diver/Operator uses tethered pod to place 6 Stabilizer Anchors in a radial pattern around the portal at prescribed depths/angles.
Each anchor requires a skill check (Engineering or Diving) to deploy; failed anchors increase sealing time.
Phase 2 — Biolock Sync & Conversion

Biologist installs 3 Biolock Nodes that inoculate portal edge with Stabilizing Mycelium protocol to reduce biological volatility and begin raw → Bio-Sap transmutation (if mission chooses Bio-Sap route).
MRM begins soft-extraction: pumps raw slurry into contained vats while Biolock Nodes modulate microflora to neutralize bleed.
Phase 3 — Seal Array Activation

Lead Engineer sets up Seal Array Kit on MRM and routes Seal Charges. AI/Net Specialist interfaces to ensure no net-echoes trigger remote instability.
Team must perform synchronized activation: Sequence = Anchor Pulse → Biolock Sync → Seal Charge Burn.
Activation requires a timed co-op sequence (window: 30s). Failure causes portal spike: extra phantoms + increased contamination; partial success lowers yield by 20–50%.
Phase 4 — Refining & Containment

If Seal succeeds: portal collapses over 1–5 minutes; raw slurry stabilizes.
MRM converts collected slurry to Refined Gel at 75–85% baseline. Optional Bio-Sap line yields 60–75% if Biolock was used.
Secure transferred Gel to fleet containers; mark Fuel Node as controlled (persistent spawn until drained).
Phase 5 — Aftermath & Cleanup

Team must plant Environmental Charges to collapse residual micro-portals (adds bonus XP/resources).
Optional: salvage signage/loot (portal-grown fauna samples, arcane net-echo fragments).
Return to nearest Harbor or mobile depot to offload Gel; distribute rewards based on contribution and seal integrity.
Failure modes & consequences

Failed Seal: portal intensifies, spawns persistent phantoms (area hazard), reduces yield potential, and increases future seal costs by 25–100%.
Partial Seal: yields smaller harvest; portal re-opens after cooldown (days).
Unregulated extraction (no seal): immediate local ecosystem corruption and high chance of temporal anomalies.
Rewards & Balancing

Base reward: Refined Gel equal to 75% of deposit size (modifiers apply).
Bonus: successful Biolock sync +10–20% Bio-Sap conversion; perfect Seal +10% extra yield, rare drop chance of Quantum Dust shard (0.25–1%).
Reputation/faction standing increases with successful controlled seals.
DESIGN NOTES FOR IN-GAME ECONOMY
Fuel Node ownership: sealing teams or controlling faction can claim Node for fixed cycle (e.g., 24–72 in-game hours) to harvest until depleted.
Market: Refined Gel sells on markets; Quantum Dust is heavily restricted and taxed; Bio-Sap trades within pastoral/biotech circles.
Seal Charges: consumable crafted in refineries or purchased; players can craft Seal Kits using refinery outputs and engineering components.
