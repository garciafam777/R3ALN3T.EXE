# R3ALN3T.EXE — Cosmos Roster
## Unranked Space Creature-Hybrids (Solar System, NetP-Hybrid Design)

---

## Design Philosophy

Same unranked, hand-placed structure as the Mythos roster — no Greek-tier grading, no card-generator stats. Each celestial body gets a small signature set of creature-hybrids, grounded in that body's real astronomical character (Mercury's heat/metal, Venus's toxic atmosphere, Mars's dust/rust, etc.) rather than generic "space alien" design, then run through the same NetP-hybrid visual rule established in the Mythos doc: circuit-trace texture worked into the natural surface, at least one point of grafted geometric plate armor per creature.

Same field-size classes carry over from the Mythos roster (S/M/L/XL) — each body gets one signature L or XL "flagship" creature plus 1–2 smaller supporting creatures, mirroring the Cerberus/Hydra-plus-smaller-roster balance from that doc.

## MERCURY — "The Forge World" (heat, metal, extreme temperature swings)

### Ferrovore (L class, flagship)
**Field footprint:** 4 panels. **Scale:** 2.4m.
A quadruped built from molten-metal plating that visibly cools and hardens on its sun-facing side, staying liquid/glowing on its shadow side — literal half-and-half material state reflecting Mercury's day/night extremes. Base tone **oxide-orange (#8A3A1A)** on the hardened half, **molten white-orange (#FFB84A)** emissive on the liquid half.

### Thermal Wisp (S class)
**Field footprint:** 1 panel. **Scale:** 0.4m.
Small floating heat-signature creature, near-invisible except for a faint emissive shimmer (#FFB84A) — swarm/harassment unit, cheap to build as a particle-heavy VFX creature rather than complex geometry.

---

## VENUS — "The Shrouded World" (toxic atmosphere, crushing pressure, acid clouds)

### Pressureback (L class, flagship)
**Field footprint:** 5 panels. **Scale:** 2.8m.
Hunched, heavily-armored quadruped — thick plate coverage reflecting the need to survive Venus's crushing atmosphere. Base **sulfurous yellow-green (#8A9A3A)**, acid-drip particle VFX at joints, emissive accent **#C9DB4A**.

### Cloudstinger (M class)
**Field footprint:** 2 panels. **Scale:** 1.5m.
Serpentine, drifts rather than walks — designed to look "dissolved" at the edges (translucent shader fading to particle static), reflecting Venus's thick cloud layer.

---

## EARTH / MOON — "The Home Pair"

### Tidebound (M class, Moon)
**Field footprint:** 3 panels. **Scale:** 1.9m.
Bipedal, pale grey-white cratered hide (#B8B4AC), moves in slow deliberate arcs (low-gravity-inspired animation timing). NetP circuit-trace especially visible here since the Moon is thematically "closest" to SkyLand's origin point — recommend the densest circuit-pattern density of any Cosmos creature.

### Regolith Crawler (S class, Moon)
**Field footprint:** 1 panel. **Scale:** 0.6m.
Small many-legged swarm unit, dusty grey (#9A968E), kicks up a visible dust-particle trail — cheap swarm-filler creature.

*(Earth itself is intentionally left without a dedicated creature — Earth is the "known" baseline the player already understands, and reserving it keeps the roster focused on the alien/unfamiliar bodies. Flag if you want an Earth-native entry added.)*

---

## MARS — "The Red World" (dust, iron oxide, thin atmosphere)

### Rustmaw (XL class, flagship)
**Field footprint:** 7 panels. **Scale:** 3.6m, sprawling low quadruped stance (echoes Cerberus's field-spanning approach from the Mythos doc rather than height).
Massive iron-oxide-red hide (#8A3A28), plated in cracked rust-textured armor, kicks up dust-storm VFX when it moves — the roster's second "field-dominant" set piece after Cerberus/Hydra, positioned as Mars's signature endgame threat.

### Duststalker (M class)
**Field footprint:** 2 panels. **Scale:** 1.6m.
Lean, fast biped, camouflaged reddish-tan (#B87A5A) — ambush-role creature, contrast to Rustmaw's slow field-dominant bulk.

---

## JUPITER — "The Storm Giant" (gas giant, Great Red Spot, massive storms)

### Stormcoil (XL class, flagship)
**Field footprint:** 6 panels, serpentine/spiral footprint (echoes the planet's storm-band visual). **Scale:** 3.2m coil diameter.
A swirling, semi-transparent coiled form — body reads as compressed storm-cloud with a solid armored "eye" core at center (direct visual reference to the Great Red Spot). Base cloud-tone banded **cream (#D8C8A8)** and **rust-red (#B85838)** stripes, core plate glowing **#FF6A3A**.

### Static Motes (S class, swarm)
**Field footprint:** 1 panel each, spawns in groups. **Scale:** 0.3m.
Small crackling energy-motes, pale yellow-white (#F0E8C8), fast and erratic — Jupiter's storm-turbulence expressed as swarm filler.

---

## SATURN — "The Ringed World"

### Ringwarden (L class, flagship)
**Field footprint:** 5 panels, unusually wide/flat footprint (a literal ring-disc silhouette around a smaller central body). **Scale:** 2.6m core, ~4m ring diameter.
Central creature body is compact and armored; the "ring" is a rotating halo of small floating ice/rock fragments orbiting it — mechanically and visually distinct from every other creature in the game (only entry with an orbiting-ring silhouette). Base core tone **pale gold-tan (#D8C098)**, ring fragments icy pale-blue (#C8E0F0).

### Ice Shard Drifter (S class)
**Field footprint:** 1 panel. **Scale:** 0.5m.
A single detached ring-fragment given minor independent life — small, crystalline, pale blue (#C8E0F0), cheap reuse of Ringwarden's ring-fragment asset as a standalone swarm unit.

---

## URANUS — "The Tilted World" (ice giant, extreme axial tilt, cold)

### Cryoglide (M class, flagship)
**Field footprint:** 3 panels. **Scale:** 2.0m.
Sleek, low-friction silhouette (reflecting the planet's smooth pale-blue-green appearance), moves in unusual sideways/rolling patterns (nod to the extreme axial tilt). Base **pale cyan-green (#A8D8C8)**, frost-crystal plate detail at joints.

---

## NEPTUNE — "The Wind World" (deepest blue, fastest winds in the solar system)

### Galewraith (L class, flagship)
**Field footprint:** 4 panels. **Scale:** 2.5m, elongated/streamlined build.
Fast, wind-swept silhouette with trailing ribbon-like fins (echoing the Elemental doc's Wind creature language, but recolored to Neptune's deep blue rather than pale green). Base **deep cobalt blue (#1A3A6A)**, emissive wind-trail accent **#4A8AD8**.

### Squall Sprite (S class, swarm)
**Field footprint:** 1 panel each. **Scale:** 0.4m.
Small fast-moving wind-elemental filler creature, pale blue-white streaks, near-transparent body.

---

## THE SUN — "The Source" (endgame-adjacent, thematically tied to signal cyan / stolen light motifs from Act III)

### Solarch (XL class, flagship — recommended true endgame capstone)
**Field footprint:** 8 panels — matches Cerberus as the largest footprint in the game, positioned as the Cosmos roster's answer to that Mythos set piece.
**Scale:** 4.5m, radiant humanoid-adjacent silhouette (not a beast — deliberately the one "elegant" entry in this roster, tonally closer to the Angelic roster than the beast-like planet creatures around it).
Base **white-gold radiant (#F4E8B8)** — the same hex as Act IV's Luminous tier and the Angelic roster's accent color, positioning Solarch as a narrative capstone tying the corruption arc (Act III's stolen cyan), the inversion arc (Act IV's white-gold), and the space arc together in one final design. Constant intense glow, no flicker (steady like Angelic units, not erratic like Demonic ones) — reinforces that this is the *true* source the whole color language has been referencing since Act I.

## UE5 Production Notes
- Recommend 3 rig families to cover this roster efficiently: quadruped (Ferrovore, Pressureback, Rustmaw, Duststalker, Cryoglide), serpentine/coil (Cloudstinger, Stormcoil), and humanoid/biped (Tidebound, Galewraith, Solarch) — swarm units (Thermal Wisp, Regolith Crawler, Static Motes, Ice Shard Drifter, Squall Sprite) can share one simple hero-prop rig with per-planet material swaps.
- Solarch and Rustmaw are this roster's two field-dominant (XL) set pieces, parallel in scale/budget to Cerberus and Hydra from the Mythos doc — plan asset budget accordingly if these are meant as trailer/marketing creatures.
- Solarch's shared hex value with Act IV's white-gold tier and the Angelic roster is intentional and load-bearing for the game's overall color-language — worth flagging to your art team so that hex doesn't get "claimed" by anything else later in production.
