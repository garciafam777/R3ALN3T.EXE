# R3ALN3T.EXE — Mythos Roster
## Unranked Endgame Virus-Hybrids (Greek Mythology, NetP-Hybrid Design)

---

## Design Philosophy

This roster sits **outside** the Tier/Element system entirely — no Greek-letter rank, no card-generator stat curve. These are unique, hand-placed endgame virus-hybrids: each one is a fusion of NetP's data-armor design language (plate/circuit/emissive logic from the base NetP doc) grown onto a classic Greek mythological creature silhouette. They read as "what happens when SkyLand's viral corruption takes the shape of legend" rather than as graded/ranked combatants.

**Field-size mechanic:** in your battle-grid system, each creature occupies a defined footprint of panels on the opposing field — from single-panel minor threats up to Cerberus, which is sized to dominate nearly the entire enemy side. Field size is treated as a core design stat here, same weight as height/build in earlier docs.

**Shared visual rule (NetP-Hybrid tell):** every creature in this roster has NetP's signature circuit-trace normal-map pattern worked into its natural texture (fur, scale, feather, or hide) and at least one point of pure geometric plate armor grafted onto an otherwise organic body — the visual signature that marks these as viral-hybrids rather than "real" mythological beasts reskinned wholesale.

---

## Field-Size Classes

| Class | Field Footprint | Approx. Scale | Design Intent |
|---|---|---|---|
| S — Minor | 1 panel | 0.3–0.8m | Swarm/harassment threats |
| M — Standard | 2–3 panels | 0.9–1.8m | Core mid-fight threats |
| L — Major | 4–6 panels | 1.9–3.5m | Dominant single-encounter threats |
| XL — Field-Dominant | 7+ panels, near-full enemy side | 3.6m+ or non-standard footprint (e.g. serpentine/sprawling) | Signature endgame set-piece creatures |

## PART 1 — XL Class (Field-Dominant)

### Cerberus (Hound of the Underworld)
**Field footprint:** 8 panels — occupies nearly the entire opposing field, the largest single-creature footprint in the game.
**Scale:** 4.2m at shoulder, ~7m nose-to-tail across all three heads/necks.
**Silhouette:** Three-headed hound, each head independently animated/targetable (three separate hitboxes — a genuine multi-head boss mechanic). Body built low and long rather than tall, reinforcing the "spans the field" footprint over verticality.
**Material:** Base hide dark charcoal (#221E1C) with NetP-signature circuit-trace pattern visible faintly through the fur, most dense around all three necks. Three collars, one per head, each glowing a different accent color (#D6283F, #D67A28, #8A28D6) — visually signals three independently-trackable danger states in the fight (e.g., each collar color could represent that head's current attack-readiness).
**Signature detail:** Chain-link plate armor (not smooth plate like NetP — heavy segmented chain, mythologically apt) draped across the shoulders and spine, grounded in Greek imagery of the underworld guardian bound in chains.

### Hydra (Lernaean Hydra)
**Field footprint:** 7 panels, serpentine — footprint extends and shifts as heads regenerate.
**Scale:** 3.8m body coil height, heads extend an additional 2–3m each.
**Silhouette:** Multi-headed serpent (recommend 5 heads as a readable, animatable number rather than mythology's variable count), body coiled rather than standing — footprint literally grows if a "regenerate" mechanic is implemented (classic Hydra gimmick: cut one head, two grow back — strong virus-battle mechanic hook).
**Material:** Deep swamp-green scale (#1C2A1A) with NetP circuit-trace worked into the scale pattern itself (glowing faintly through translucent scale edges), accent emissive **#6ADB6A** at each head's throat when about to attack (clear telegraph).
**Signature detail:** Each regenerated head is slightly more corrupted/glitchy-looking than the last (subtle escalating VFX per regrowth) — mechanically reinforces "don't just hack at it."

## PART 2 — L Class (Major)

### Minotaur
**Field footprint:** 5 panels. **Scale:** 2.6m.
Bull-headed humanoid, NetP-style plate armor covering torso/shoulders in **dark iron (#2E1A1C)**, circuit-trace worked into the fur on the bull-head/neck. Carries an oversized labyrinth-motif axe (geometric maze pattern etched into the blade — direct lore tie-in) that glows **#FF5A1F** along its edge.

### Chimera
**Field footprint:** 5 panels (irregular — lion body + goat head mid-back + serpent tail head, three attack points). **Scale:** 2.8m body length.
Lion-bodied, goat-headed second maw, serpent-tailed. Each of the three "faces" gets a different elemental accent tying into your Elemental system's palette: lion head **#FF5A1F** (fire, matching myth), goat head **#8ADB2A** (poison-ish, corrupted-nature read), serpent tail **#6A28D6** (dark). This creature is a natural bridge between the Mythos and Elemental docs if you want a crossover encounter.

### Nemean Lion
**Field footprint:** 4 panels. **Scale:** 2.4m.
Massive lion, hide rendered as literal plate armor (mythologically the hide was impenetrable — here it's rendered as NetP-style overlapping plate scales fused directly into the fur silhouette, rather than fur at all on the torso). Base tone **burnished gold-brown (#8A6B3A)**, emissive circuit-veins **#E8D48A** visible only when the hide is "struck" (a fitting visual for an invincible-hide gimmick fight).

### Talos
**Field footprint:** 5 panels. **Scale:** 3.2m — the most literally "machine" entry in the roster.
Bronze automaton — the one creature in Greek mythology that's already partially mechanical, making it the cleanest NetP-hybrid conceptually. Full plate coverage, **brushed bronze (#8A5A2A)** with **#FFB84A** emissive seams, visible single "ichor vein" (glowing molten line) running ankle-to-neck — mythologically Talos's single vulnerability was a vein sealed by a pin at the ankle, a strong boss-mechanic callback if you want a literal weak-point fight.

### Sphinx
**Field footprint:** 4 panels. **Scale:** 2.3m (body length ~3.5m).
Lion body, human-presenting head/torso, feathered wings. Head/torso rendered with NetP's own face-material logic (subsurface skin shader) contrasted against a lion body in **sandstone tan (#C9A96A)**. Wings feathered, tipped in **#E8D48A**. Strong candidate for a riddle/puzzle-gimmick encounter rather than pure combat, fitting its myth.

## PART 3 — M Class (Standard)

### Medusa (Gorgon)
**Field footprint:** 3 panels. **Scale:** 1.9m.
Humanoid, snake-hair (a dozen small independently-animated serpent strands rather than a solid headdress — more readable at combat speed). Base skin tone shifted to pale stone-grey-green (#8A9A8A) — foreshadows her petrification ability. Serpent hair strands each carry the NetP circuit-trace pattern faintly.

### Griffin
**Field footprint:** 3 panels. **Scale:** 1.8m at shoulder (wingspan ~4m).
Eagle head/wings, lion body. **Golden-tan (#C9A96A)** body, **white-gold (#F4E8B8)** wing accents — deliberately echoes Act IV's Luminous palette, positioning Griffin as a "noble" rather than "monstrous" entry if you want tonal variety within the roster.

### Manticore
**Field footprint:** 3 panels. **Scale:** 2.0m body length.
Lion body, human-presenting face, scorpion tail. Base **deep red hide (#8A0F1F)** — deliberately reuses the Demonic roster's oxidized-red anchor color, positioning Manticore as tonally adjacent to that faction without literally being Demonic-type.

### Centaur
**Field footprint:** 2 panels. **Scale:** 2.1m at shoulder.
Horse body, human torso. NetP-style plate on the humanoid torso only, leaving the equine half unarmored/natural — a clean half-and-half read reinforcing the hybrid concept simply through armor placement.

### Harpy
**Field footprint:** 2 panels. **Scale:** 1.4m.
Bird-woman, feathered wings and lower body, humanoid upper torso and face. Dark grey-brown plumage (#3A342E), talons rendered with sharp metallic plate tips.

### Siren
**Field footprint:** 2 panels. **Scale:** 1.6m.
Human-presenting upper body, avian lower half (classical Greek siren, not the later mermaid conflation) — keeps it visually distinct from any water-themed Elemental variant. Plumage in muted sea-grey (#5A6A6E), voice-themed emissive throat-glow tying thematically to the Elemental doc's "Sound" element accent color (#D46AFF) if you want a direct crossover reference.

### Satyr
**Field footprint:** 2 panels. **Scale:** 1.5m.
Goat-legged humanoid. Minimal armor (a single NetP-style shoulder plate, otherwise bare) — smallest armor footprint of any Mythos entry, fitting a lesser/mischievous-threat role rather than a heavyweight.

### Cretan Bull
**Field footprint:** 3 panels. **Scale:** 1.9m at shoulder.
Standard bull silhouette, oversized. Base hide **dark umber (#3A2418)**, single NetP plate-collar around the neck as the only armor element — deliberately minimal, since myth doesn't give this creature much beyond raw size/aggression.

## PART 4 — S Class (Minor / Swarm)

### Stymphalian Bird (swarm unit — spawns in groups of 4–6)
**Field footprint:** 1 panel each. **Scale:** 0.5m.
Bronze-feathered bird, metallic plumage rendered as tiny overlapping plate-scales. Base **#8A5A2A** (echoing Talos's bronze, mythologically linked). Small, fast, ranged (feather-dart) attacker — classic swarm-filler role.

### Empusa (swarm/ambush unit)
**Field footprint:** 1 panel. **Scale:** 1.3m.
Shapeshifter-themed — recommend a flickering/glitching material treatment (unstable emissive, similar to the Plasma element's irregular pulse) as the visual language for its shapeshifting myth trait, cheap to build as a shader effect rather than actual model-swapping.

### Lamia
**Field footprint:** 1 panel. **Scale:** 1.4m.
Serpent-lower-body hybrid, humanoid upper torso. Pale, gaunt coloring (#9A8A8A), minimal armor — a "lesser threat that looks unsettling rather than powerful" role, good pacing contrast against the L/XL set-piece fights.

### Ichthyocentaur
**Field footprint:** 1 panel. **Scale:** 1.3m (aquatic-adjacent unit, if your field system supports water panels).
Fish-tailed centaur variant — human torso, horse forelegs, fish tail. Deep teal hide (#1A3A3E), scale-plate detail. Useful if any Act has a water-themed zone needing Mythos-roster filler.

## Roster Totals
- XL Class: 2 (Cerberus, Hydra)
- L Class: 6 (Minotaur, Chimera, Nemean Lion, Talos, Sphinx)
- M Class: 8 (Medusa, Griffin, Manticore, Centaur, Harpy, Siren, Satyr, Cretan Bull)
- S Class: 4 (Stymphalian Bird, Empusa, Lamia, Ichthyocentaur)
- **Total: 20 unique Mythos creatures**

This covers the core, widely-recognized Greek mythological creature roster at a curated, production-sane count rather than an exhaustive catalog of every named beast in Greek myth (which runs into the hundreds, mostly one-off/obscure). Flag any specific additional creatures (e.g. Scylla, Charybdis, Typhon, Echidna, Argus Panoptes) if you want the roster expanded — several of those would make strong additional XL/L entries if you want more endgame set-piece variety.

## UE5 Production Notes
- Unlike the Angelic/Demonic/Antagonist rosters, this set does **not** share 2 skeletons — mythological creature variety (biped, quadruped, serpentine, avian, hybrid multi-limb) genuinely requires distinct rig families. Recommend grouping into 4 rig types: humanoid-hybrid (Minotaur, Medusa, Centaur, Satyr, Manticore-face, Sphinx-face), quadruped (Nemean Lion, Griffin, Chimera-body, Cretan Bull), serpentine (Hydra, Lamia, Ichthyocentaur-tail), and avian (Harpy, Siren, Stymphalian Bird).
- Cerberus and Hydra (XL class) are your two biggest asset investments in this roster — budget accordingly, as their field-dominant footprint means they're likely to be signature marketing/trailer creatures.
- Field-footprint values above assume a standard BN-style grid; adjust panel counts directly if your actual grid dimensions differ from a typical 3×6 per side.
