1. The Sublimation Tuner (The Rig Driver / Heavy Tech)
This career focuses on large-scale, brute-force industrial processing. Tuners don't sit in labs; they use vehicles like the Ridgeback-Class Rig or the Stratus-6 as mobile, roaring refineries.

The Bugmatter Loop: They collect raw industrial scrap and run it through modified engine manifolds or unshielded induction rails, forcing heavy Crust-Matter to sublime out of the exhaust wake.

Gameplay Integration: * High-Volume Logistics: They accept high-risk hauling contracts, using the weight of their armored, Bugmatter-clad rigs to plow through blockades.

Hardware Fabricator: They convert Crust-Matter into heavy, physical components—like armored plating, anti-scan shielding, and high-density server rack enclosures for local networks.

Risk Factor: Managing the literal heat. Pushing the engine too hard to synthesize high-grade matter risks cooking the cargo or blowing a thruster manifold mid-transit.

2. The Logic Splicer (The Software Architect)
Splicers treat Bugmatter as raw computational clay. They work entirely in the clean zones, using precise software exploits to force data to condense into material objects.

The Bugmatter Loop: They write infinite, recursive C++ loops and intentionally execute un-compiled, high-dimensional arrays to trigger local memory sub-system failures, harvesting pristine Soot-Matter directly from server heatsinks.

Gameplay Integration:

Firmware Optimization: Splicers use Soot-Matter to physically coat local processing nodes, massively boosting calculation speeds and data throughput for their networks.

Exploit Development: They specialize in forging physical "glitch keys" or injection drives that can bypass high-level corporate security grids by overloading physical lock mechanisms with corrupted logic.

Risk Factor: Code instability. A poorly optimized script can corrupt the very hardware they are using to synthesize the matter, forcing local system restores.

3. The Navi-Cultivator (The Bio-Tech Netrunner)
Cultivators occupy the eerie space where digital intelligence meets biological form. They use rogue NetNavi data streams as genetic blueprints to grow living computer systems.

The Bugmatter Loop: They capture anomalous NetNavi code vectors from the deep Net and pipe them into conductive synthetic gel matrices, growing glowing, semi-fluid Vessel-Matter.

Gameplay Integration:

Organic Hardware Engineering: They grow custom, living processing units that don't rely on standard silicon. These organic components can adapt to network attacks in real-time.

Navi Modification: They use Vessel-Matter to physically graft upgrades onto their digital NetNavi characters, unlocking hidden sub-routines and higher-dimensional processing traits.

Risk Factor: Sentient bleed. If the NetNavi code used to seed the culture is too unstable, the resulting Vessel-Matter can attempt to rewrite the local terminal's host operating system to escape.

4. The Grid Scavenger (The Street Operator)
Scavengers are the survivalists of the lower sectors. They don't have corporate labs or expensive rigs; they rely on the waste of the upper city to survive.

The Bugmatter Loop: They set up passive induction nets and static collectors under corporate sky-bridges, scraping toxic Sludge-Matter out of the atmospheric runoff and corporate data vents.

Gameplay Integration:

Improvised Chemistry: They filter volatile Sludge-Matter into cheap, hyper-combustible fuel variants for low-tier thrusters or use it as a crude, black-market coating that masks vehicle signatures from automated patrol grids.

Underground Trade: They act as the foundational suppliers of raw material to the other careers, trading bulk unrefined sludge for high-tier hardware or optimized software tools.

Risk Factor: High environmental toxicity. Handling unrefined atmospheric runoff without proper shielding or filtration slowly degrades physical gear and causes local system glitches.
